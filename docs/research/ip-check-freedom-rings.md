# IP Research — Freedom Rings
**Type:** Trademark & IP Check  
**Status:** Researched — Strong Recommendation  
**Decision:** PENDING — phrase selection (what CTA/language FFP uses for its rings)  
**Researched:** 2026-04-15  
**Researcher:** CTO (Claude Code CTO Mode)

---

## Scope

Three questions going into pre-launch:

1. Is the name **"Freedom Rings"** clear for use in a personal finance platform?
2. Does the **ring/circular progress chart visual concept** create any IP exposure?
3. Is the phrase **"Close the Rings"** available, or does Apple own it?

---

## Findings

### 1. "Freedom Rings" — the Name

**Status: Clear (with one verification step pending)**

No registered trademark was found for "Freedom Rings" in fintech, personal finance, or software categories. Nearby names in the space:

- *Freedom Finance* — UK digital lending marketplace (different use case, different geography)
- *FinTech Freedom* — media/content brand (no ring concept, no conflict)

Neither conflicts with a ring-based financial planning tool operating under the Financial Freedom Platform brand.

**Required before public launch:** Run a direct search at [tmsearch.uspto.gov](https://tmsearch.uspto.gov/) for "Freedom Rings" across all International Classes. Web research returned no conflicting registrations, but a live USPTO check is the authoritative confirmation. Takes 5 minutes.

---

### 2. Ring / Circular Progress Chart Visual Concept

**Status: No exposure**

Apple's Activity Rings trademark (filed August 26, 2015, Registration granted June 14, 2016) is registered under **International Class 009** with scope explicitly limited to:

> *"computer software for tracking, storing, viewing, monitoring, displaying, transmitting and managing user interaction data concerning exercise, physical activity, steps taken, distance walked or run, time standing or sitting, calories consumed, calories burned; computer software for tracking, monitoring and managing information and goals regarding a health and fitness program"*

This is a health and fitness classification. Personal finance is a separate International Class entirely. Ring charts, donut charts, and circular progress indicators are a generic, widely-used UI pattern across analytics, finance, dashboards, and productivity software — none of which fall under Apple's registered scope.

**Conclusion:** FFP's use of ring charts for financial metrics (Debt, Income, Savings, Retirement, Legacy) carries no IP exposure against Apple's Activity Rings trademark.

---

### 3. "Close the Rings" — the Phrase

**Status: Off limits. Strong recommendation not to use.**

Apple holds registered trademark protection for **"Close Your Rings"**:

- US trademark: granted June 14, 2016 (figurative mark)
- EU trademark (EUIPO): filed May 16, 2019
- International Classes: 9, 10, 14, 44 — covering software, medical apparatus, watches/horological instruments, and medical services

"Close the Rings" is sufficiently close to "Close Your Rings" that Apple would have a strong argument for consumer confusion, particularly as Apple Watch and its ring metaphor become increasingly mainstream. Any phrase in the pattern "\_\_\_ the/your Rings" that implies a completion action carries risk.

**Do not use:**
- "Close the Rings"
- "Close Your Rings"
- Any direct echo of Apple's completion-action framing

---

## Strong Recommendation

**Use the name "Freedom Rings" — it is clear.** The visual concept is safe. The only constraint is the CTA/action phrase.

FFP needs its own ring language — distinct from Apple's completion metaphor and owned by the platform. Candidate directions (none are final — decision pending):

| Phrase | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| "Fill Your Rings" | Action verb, distinct from Apple's "close" framing |
| "Build Your Rings" | Growth-oriented, fits financial planning context |
| "Check Your Rings" | Informational, low conflict risk |
| "Your Freedom Score" | Leans into the platform brand, rings are the visual vehicle |
| "Level Up Your Rings" | More gamified, suits younger audience |

The chosen phrase should be run through the same USPTO check before it appears in any public-facing marketing or UI.
