April 2026
🆕 New Module — v1.0

PIN lock — content-level security

First FFP module with a dedicated PIN lock separate from the profile Recovery PIN. 4-digit entry with individual boxes and auto-advance. Auto-locks when the tab is hidden. Recovery path via profile Recovery PIN. Built as a reusable usePinLock hook for future modules.

Legacy Ring — Wealth Ring feed for the Dashboard

calcLegacyHealth() computes a 0-100 score from four coverage dimensions: Life (40%), Disability (30%), Health binary (20%), Auto binary (10%). Score is written to ins_legacy_health_{profileId} on every policy save and loaded by the Dashboard for the Legacy Ring.

5-tab policy vault

Coverage tab shows policy cards by type with a coverage summary bar (gap vs target). Beneficiaries tab consolidates all named beneficiaries across policies. Emergency Info tab is print-ready for family access — carrier, agent, policy number, and beneficiaries in one clean layout. Analysis tab has three AI features. Settings tab manages PIN.

3 AI analysis features

Coverage Gap Analysis identifies underinsured areas and estimates monthly cost to close each gap. Term vs Permanent Life gives a personalized recommendation based on age, debt load, and retirement trajectory. Quote Comparison calculates cost-per-$1K coverage across up to 3 carrier quotes. All results persisted with timestamps.

Approximate coverage flag

Employer group benefits estimated as a salary multiple (1x, 2x, 3x) are tagged isApproximate: true and displayed with a ~ prefix. Tooltip: "Approximate — based on your salary data." Approximate amounts are included in Legacy Ring calculations with an advisory note.

Expiry warnings

Policy cards flag upcoming expirations: amber at 90 days, red at 30 days. Term life and disability policies most commonly trigger this.

🆕 New Module — v1.0

Freedom Score — cross-module health composite

A single 0-100 score synthesized from all six live modules: Debt (30%), Income (20%), Spending (20%), Savings (20%), Retirement (10%). Below 100 renders Freedom Rings. At 100+ the rings shift to Wealth Rings with a different color palette.

Collapsible sidebar with per-module health rings

Collapsed (52px) shows icon + SVG arc ring per module. Expanded (200px) adds module name and primary metric amount. Each ring is an independent health calculation — debt paydown %, income stability %, spending within budget %, goals funded %, retirement progress %, and investment portfolio gain.

4 flanking stat tiles

Net Position (savings + retirement − debt), Income (monthly normalized), Debt change % this month, and Savings goals funded. Each tile clicks through to the relevant module.

4 dynamic alert cards

Priority-ranked alerts across all modules: promo APR expiring within 30 days, unreviewed transactions, savings goals due within 30 days (green if funded, amber if short), retirement target below spending baseline, and income up/down more than 5%.

6 live charts

Top row: Over Budget (top 5 categories vs baseline average), Income Trend (3-month sparkline), Debt-to-Income gauge (semicircle, green/amber/red). Bottom row: Savings Goals progress bars, Retirement Ring with 4 stat tiles, Monthly P&L Waterfall with Category/Account toggle and step-up bars for savings and retirement contributions.

Graceful degradation — every tile and chart

If a module hasn't been used yet, its tile or chart shows an empty state with a "Launch [Module] to get started" button. The dashboard works from day one, even on a fresh profile with no data. Freedom Score defaults to a partial calculation from whichever modules have data.

🆕 New Module — v1.0

Fund + goal two-layer model

A Fund represents a physical savings account. Goals live inside it as named line items — each with its own target amount, progress bar, and due date. One account can hold multiple goals (car registration, home insurance, vacation) without needing separate bank accounts.

Two goal types — Accumulation and Sinking Fund

Accumulation goals are for open-ended saving toward a target (emergency fund, down payment). Sinking fund goals are for predictable recurring expenses — the app calculates the exact monthly contribution needed based on target amount and due date.

Calculated monthly contribution

For any goal with a due date, the app shows the required monthly contribution: (target − saved) ÷ months remaining. You can override it with a custom amount — the calculated value stays visible as a reference so you always know if you're on track.

30-day alert system

When a goal's due date is within 30 days, an alert banner appears automatically at the top of the app. Green if you're fully funded and ready to pay. Amber if you're short — with the exact shortfall amount so you know what to transfer before the bill hits.

SpendingTracker integration — automatic suggestions

On first load, Savings scans your SpendingTracker transactions for recurring annual and irregular expenses (tagged with Recurrence Type). It groups them by pattern and suggests them as sinking fund goals — one tap to add with the target amount and category pre-filled.

Mark-as-paid detection

When a sinking fund bill shows up in SpendingTracker, Savings detects it automatically on load and prompts you to mark the goal as paid. Confirming resets the goal's progress to zero and restarts the accumulation cycle — no manual reset needed.

Deposit and contribution logging

Log deposits at the fund level as money moves into your account. Log contributions per goal to track how much of the fund is allocated toward each target. Fund balance and goal progress update immediately.

JSON + CSV backup and restore

Full JSON backup exports funds and goals together. CSV export produces separate files for funds and goals — open in Excel or Google Sheets. Both formats support Replace or Merge on import.

✨ New Feature — v1.9

Receipt scan — camera and photo library

Tap 📷 Scan Receipt in the Transactions tab to capture or upload receipt images. Uses Claude's vision model to extract merchant name, date, total, and every line item. Works on mobile (rear camera) and desktop (file picker). Multiple image files can be selected at once.

Multiple receipts per image

One photo can contain multiple receipts — Claude finds and extracts them all. Results are consolidated into a single review screen regardless of how many receipts or images were submitted.

Review screen — edit before confirming

Every extracted receipt appears as an editable card: merchant name, date, total, and per-line-item category dropdowns. AI-suggested categories are pre-filled. Low-confidence items (blank description or zero amount) are flagged in amber for manual review. Nothing is saved until you confirm.

Duplicate detection and reconciliation prompt

Before saving a scanned receipt, SpendingTracker checks existing transactions for a match (same amount, date within 3 days, similar merchant name). If a match is found, a side-by-side comparison appears: Skip (mark existing as reconciled), Replace (swap old with new), or Keep Both. Prevents double-counting when you scan before your bank statement imports.

Entry method tracking

All transactions now carry an entryMethod field: "scan" for receipt scans, "csv_import" for bank statement imports, "manual" for hand-entered transactions. Additive — no changes to existing stored data.
