Getting Started
1

Add Your Retirement Accounts

Tap + Add Account in the Accounts tab. Select the type, enter the current balance and your annual contribution. For 401k/403b accounts, add your contribution rate (% of salary) and employer match — this significantly affects your projection.

2

Enter Your Social Security Estimate

In the Overview tab, enter your estimated monthly Social Security benefit from ssa.gov/estimator and the age you plan to claim. This counts as guaranteed income and reduces the nest egg you need — don't skip it.

3

Set Your Retirement Target

Enter your current age, target retirement age, annual salary, and the monthly income you want in retirement. These feed the projection math. Smart defaults handle the rest — return rate (7%), inflation (3%), salary growth (3%) are pre-set and explained.

4

Pick and Lock a Withdrawal Plan

Go to the Plan & AI tab. Review the side-by-side comparison of all 4 withdrawal plans. Find the one that matches your situation and tap Lock This Plan. All projections, the on-track gauge, and AI analysis use the locked plan as the target.

The 4% rule and 25x rule are the same math. 4% withdrawal = you need 25× your annual spending. We call it the 4% Rule because that's how you think about it in retirement — "I'll withdraw 4% per year."
Projections & Assumptions
📊

30-Year Projection Chart

SVG chart from today to your retirement age. Your projected balance (solid line) vs the target nest egg for your locked plan (dashed line). Shaded area shows surplus or gap at every age.

🎯

On-Track Gauge

The Overview tab shows a large green/amber/red gauge: are you on track, close, or behind? Plus four stats: projected balance, required nest egg, monthly gap/surplus, and years to retirement.

🎚️

Assumption Sliders

Adjust expected return (1–15%), inflation (0–8%), and salary growth (0–10%) with sliders. Each has a plain-language explanation. Changes update the projection chart instantly — use them to stress-test your plan.

Default assumptions: 7% return (historical US stock market average), 3% inflation (long-term US average), 3% salary growth. These are reasonable starting points — adjust for your situation.
AI Analysis
🧠

Retirement Readiness Assessment

Tap Analyze My Retirement in the Plan & AI tab. AI reads all your accounts, your locked plan, your projection, and your surplus/gap. Returns a direct verdict — on track, ahead, or behind — plus three specific recommendations.

For the best analysis: Lock a withdrawal plan, enter your Social Security estimate, and make sure all accounts have accurate balances and contribution rates before running the analysis. The AI is only as accurate as the data you give it.
Tips
🏦

Don't forget employer match

Employer match is free money. If your employer matches 3% of salary, contributing less than that leaves money on the table. The projection math includes full match — make sure you've set it correctly.

📋

Get your SS estimate

Visit ssa.gov/estimator to get your personalized Social Security benefit estimate. It's free and takes 2 minutes. Even a rough estimate significantly improves the accuracy of your on-track assessment.

🔒

Lock your plan

Until you lock a withdrawal plan, you're planning toward a moving target. Pick one — even if you change it later — so your on-track gauge and AI analysis have a clear benchmark to work with.
