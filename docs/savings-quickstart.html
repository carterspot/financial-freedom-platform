🏦 Quick Start Guide · v1.0

Get started with
Savings Module

Track savings funds and goals — from emergency funds to sinking funds for annual bills. Never be caught off guard by a predictable expense again.

How It Works
🏦

Funds = Physical Accounts

A Fund represents a real savings account (e.g. "Ally Savings"). You name it, give it a color, and log deposits as money moves in. The fund tracks your running balance.

🎯

Goals = Line Items Inside a Fund

Goals live inside a fund — like buckets of money earmarked for specific purposes. Car registration, emergency fund, vacation — each is a separate goal with its own target and progress.

📊

Fund Balance vs Goal Allocations

Each fund shows total balance vs the sum of goal allocations. A progress bar turns red if your goals exceed what's in the account.

Two Goal Types
Accumulation

Manual savings

You control contributions. Use for:

  • Emergency fund
  • Vacation
  • Down payment
Sinking Fund

Predictable bills

Tied to a known recurring expense. App calculates required monthly contribution. Use for:

  • Car registration
  • Home insurance
  • Annual subscriptions
Monthly contribution is calculated automatically: (Target − Saved) ÷ Months until due date. You can override it if you want to contribute more or less.
Getting Started
1

Create Your First Fund

Tap + Add Fund at the bottom of the screen. Give it a name (e.g. "Sinking Fund Account"), an optional account nickname (e.g. "Ally Savings"), and a color. The fund is your container — goals go inside it.

2

Add a Goal Inside the Fund

Tap + Add Goal inside a fund card. Choose Accumulation or Sinking Fund, set a target amount, and add a due date. For sinking funds, the app shows you the required monthly contribution automatically.

3

Log a Deposit to the Fund

When you transfer money into your savings account, tap + Log Deposit on the fund card. Enter the amount and an optional note. The fund balance updates immediately.

4

Log a Contribution to a Goal

When you allocate part of a deposit toward a specific goal, tap Log Contribution on the goal row. This tracks how much of your fund is earmarked for each goal and updates the progress bar.

SpendingTracker Integration
💡

Automatic Sinking Fund Suggestions

On first run, Savings scans your SpendingTracker transactions for recurring annual or irregular expenses (marked with the recurrence tags you set). It groups them and suggests them as sinking fund goals — one tap to add.

To get better suggestions: In SpendingTracker, edit your recurring bills (car insurance, registration, etc.) and set their Recurrence Type to Annual, Quarterly, or Semi-Annual. Savings reads these tags.
✅

Mark as Paid Detection

When a sinking fund bill shows up in your SpendingTracker transactions, Savings detects it on load and prompts you: "Looks like Car Registration was paid — mark as paid and reset cycle?"

🔄

Cycle Reset

Confirming the payment resets the goal's progress to zero and records the pay date. The monthly contribution recalculates automatically for the next cycle.

Alert System

When a goal has a due date within 30 days, an alert banner appears at the top of the app:

✅
Car Registration is fully funded and due in 12 days. You're ready to pay.
⚠️
Home Insurance is due in 18 days — $240 short. Consider making an extra contribution.
No action needed to set up alerts. They appear automatically based on goal due dates. No extra storage keys or configuration required.
Backup & Restore
💾

JSON Full Backup

Exports all funds and goals together in a single file. Use Restore Backup to reload — choose Replace (overwrites everything) or Merge (appends new records, skips duplicates by ID).

📄

CSV Export / Import

Export funds and goals as separate CSV files — open in Excel or Google Sheets. Import CSVs back in with the same Replace or Merge options. Useful for bulk edits outside the app.

Tips
🏦

One fund, many goals

You don't need a separate savings account for each goal. One sinking fund account can hold multiple goals — just keep the total allocations under the fund balance.

📅

Set due dates early

Even rough due dates (e.g. December for annual bills) give the app enough signal to calculate a monthly contribution and fire alerts. You can refine them anytime.

🔗

Tag bills in SpendingTracker first

Before setting up Savings, go to SpendingTracker and tag your recurring annual/quarterly bills with a Recurrence Type. Savings will auto-suggest them as sinking fund goals on first load.
